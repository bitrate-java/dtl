The Problem Space

Challenges in PV Manufacturing

Solar photovoltaic module manufacturing requires high-precision machinery operating under massive throughput requirements. Equipment degradation severely limits productivity.

01

Unexpected Equipment Failure

Sudden failures in critical motors, conveyor belts, or lamination units lead to immediate line shutdowns without warning, rendering reactive maintenance highly inefficient.

02

Production Downtime

PV assembly lines are highly synchronized sequential setups. A single component breakdown stalls the entire throughput, resulting in massive operational backlogs.

03

High Maintenance Costs

Replacing complex components under emergency conditions incurs expedited shipment fees, dynamic contract premiums, and severe collateral damage to adjacent machines.

04

Reduced Throughput

Operating degraded components at sub-optimal speeds to prevent failure reduces production volume and causes variations in solar cell lamination quality.

05

Manual Inspection Limitations

Traditional inspection methods rely heavily on periodic calendar schedules and human auditory or visual checks, failing to spot microscopic sub-surface cracks or thermal fatigue.

Strategic Vision

Project Evolution Roadmap

From basic software simulation algorithms to full scale hardware and production-ready IIoT deployments.

PHASE 1

MATLAB Simulation

Designing physical plant model in Simulink. Injecting structural degradations to export high-fidelity validation data datasets.

PHASE 2

Hardware Prototype

Assembling a micro-scale conveyor model with integrated DC motors, bearings, and temperature modules to mimic a real assembly track.

PHASE 3

ESP32 Sensor Integration

Deploying high-speed ESP32 boards equipped with ADXL345 vibration sensors and DS18B20 temperature probes to pipe analog telemetry.

PHASE 4

Cloud-Based Monitoring

Configuring MQTT brokers to route sensor payloads securely into cloud-based databases for continuous remote accessibility.

PHASE 5

Mobile Application

Developing lightweight responsive diagnostic applications for factory engineers to receive real-time push alerts on equipment states.

PHASE 6

Digital Twin Technology

Synthesizing 3D WebGL plant representations displaying synchronized spatial sensor mapping in response to real-time physical telemetry.

PHASE 7

Industrial Deployment

Adapting interface protocols (Modbus, OPC UA) to pipe telemetry directly from production lines into our ML diagnostic pipelines.
